# Specialization Overview: Build with Open Models and Tools (Optional)

This 13 course Coursera Original specialization is designed for engineers and technical professionals who want to build, fine-tune, deploy, and maintain open AI systems. Each course is short, practical, and builds toward production-level skills. Complete the specialization to earn a Certificate in Open Generative AI: Build with Open Models and Tools, demonstrating applied expertise with open models and tools.

## Course 1: Foundations of Open Generative AI Engineering

Understand “open” (open source, open weights, open access) to select models that won’t block deployment.

Compare architectures like LLaMA, Mistral, and diffusion models to match the right tool to text, image, or multimodal tasks.

Build a decision framework for open vs. closed models to reduce compliance risks and prevent costly rework.

Why it matters: You will establish a framework for selecting the right models, avoid compliance risks, and prepare effectively for deployment.

## Course 2: Understanding Open AI Workspaces

Set up and run local LLMs with Ollama to keep sensitive data under your control during experiments.

Create reproducible environments with Docker to ensure results stay consistent across teams.

Use  Jupyter Notebook GPUs for training and inference to scale projects affordably without heavy infrastructure.

Why it matters: You will create a reliable environment for experimentation, safeguard sensitive data, and ensure reproducible results across teams.

## Course 3: Preparing Text for AI Models

Source and preprocess datasets from Hugging Face, Kaggle, and Common Crawl to ensure model inputs are reliable.

Format data for instruction tuning and fine-tuning to make outputs align with project needs.

Apply quality checks that prevent small errors from compounding in production models.

Why it matters: You will transform raw text into clean, structured datasets that improve model accuracy and performance.

## Course 4: Preparing Images for AI Models

Collect and evaluate datasets like LAION or ImageNet to strengthen training reliability.

Apply preprocessing and augmentation to handle real-world image variation.

Structure datasets effectively to save time and resources in diffusion model training.

Why it matters: You will prepare and organize image datasets that strengthen training outcomes and conserve resources.

## Course 5: Fine-tuning Text Models with PEFT

Apply PEFT methods such as LoRA and QLoRA to fine-tune without requiring large compute budgets.

Optimize training to run efficiently on consumer hardware.

Evaluate fine-tuned models with metrics like ROUGE and BERTScore to verify measurable gains.

Why it matters: You will apply efficient fine-tuning methods that reduce costs and confirm measurable improvements.

## Course 6: Fine-tuning Image Models with Diffusion

Break down diffusion architectures (Stable Diffusion, U-Net, VAE) to know where adjustments can be made.

Personalize models with DreamBooth to generate outputs tailored to a domain or brand.

Use ComfyUI to streamline workflow design and accelerate iteration.

Why it matters: You will customize diffusion workflows to accelerate iteration and produce outputs tailored to your domain.

## Course 7: Model Evaluation and Benchmarking

Apply text metrics (Bilingual Evaluation Understudy, Recall-Oriented Understudy for Gisting Evaluation) and image metrics (FID, CLIP) to measure performance with precision.

Combine automated evaluation with human review to build trust in results.

Build reproducible benchmarking frameworks to compare models consistently across projects.

Why it matters: You will implement reliable evaluation methods that ensure fairness, reproducibility, and trust in results.

## Course 8: Optimizing Models for Production

Apply quantization (INT8, INT4) to shrink models while maintaining accuracy.

Optimize inference pipelines to deliver low-latency performance.

Tune deployments to align with available hardware and production needs.

Why it matters: You will deliver faster, lighter deployments while maintaining accuracy and system stability.

## Course 9: Building Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Systems with Open Models

Design retrieval-augmented generation pipelines to ground outputs in current data.

Use vector databases like FAISS and ChromaDB for scalable retrieval.

Mitigate hallucinations to deliver more accurate, trustworthy results.

Why it matters: You will ground outputs in current data, reduce hallucinations, and provide answers that can be trusted.

## Course 10: API Development and Model Serving

Build REST APIs with FastAPI to integrate models directly into applications.

Apply Model Context Protocol (MCP) concepts to connect models with external tools.

Scale APIs with containers and load balancing to handle production traffic reliably.

Why it matters: You will build scalable APIs that integrate models directly into production applications.

## Course 11: Agent Development Fundamentals

Understand agent types, reactive, deliberative, and hybrid, to design systems suited to different tasks.

Implement tool-calling and structured prompting to extend agent capabilities.

Add memory systems that support agents handling long-running interactions.

Why it matters: You will design agents with reasoning, tool use, and memory to support extended, practical interactions.

## Course 12: Deploying Open Models

Containerize and deploy with Docker and cloud platforms to transition smoothly from lab to production.

Compare cost and compliance trade-offs across providers before scaling.

Monitor, log, and maintain deployments to ensure stability and compliance over time.

Why it matters: You will deploy models securely, manage costs effectively, and ensure long-term maintainability.

## Course 13: Ethics and Safety in Open AI

Detect and mitigate bias in data and outputs to reduce reputational and legal risks.

Build safety layers and moderation systems to protect end users.

Implement provenance, watermarking, and licensing checks to safeguard compliant use.

Why it matters: You will implement safeguards that address bias, ensure safety, and maintain licensing compliance.

A recognized certificate in Open Generative AI Engineering that demonstrates applied, production-ready skills.

Hands-on experience with the core tools used across the industry.

A portfolio of real projects, fine-tuned models, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems, Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) and safety audits, to showcase your expertise.
